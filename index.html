About Me

My name is Evan Brill. I am a sophomore computer science/software engineering major at Rose-Hulman Institute of technology. My particular interests include C programming and binary exploitation in the ARM architecture. However, I have recently been branching out into other architectures and undertaking more complex projects. I also do a fair bit of high level development in languages like Java and Python as it is required for school. I occasionally use these languages to do work on my own projects or write silly things like Minecraft mods.

I am also a member of the RHIT cross country and track and field teams. This takes up the vast majority of my time outside of class and schoolwork, but I really enjoy it so I am okay with that. In the rare times where I am not running or working on school, I like to spend my time outdoors. I do a fair bit of fishing, hiking, kayaking, biking, and camping, especially in the summers.
